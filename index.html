About Me

I joined a research lab straight out of my undergraduate education, and this has set me on a career trajectory I never could have imagined. After four years of analyzing behavioral data collected from rodents (some of which I had performed brain surgery on, others I had administered heroin to) I realized that the life of a research scientist was not for me. I began working as an analyst in the billing department of one of the largest private labs in the greater NYC area, and then managed a moving company in the up and coming Greenpoint area of Brooklyn. This job tested my resolve in so many different ways, and I'm proud to say that while I was manager of the company we saw exceptional growth across the board. Having a role which allowed me to evaluate a situation and be a part of the decision-making process gave me a sense of accomplishment, and I crave more of this. After a few years I decided that to get to where I want to be more tools would be needed. I returned to Brooklyn College to study big-data and multivariate statistics, and built upon this by receivng an MS from Fordham Gabelli Business School. Throughout both post-graduate degrees till today I've been teaching 50-125 students per semester (mostly statistics or experimental psychology)at BC. During the lockdown I was accepted into The Data Incubator, an in-depth hands-on experence wherein there were challenges presented that a data scientist experiences in real-world situations. Completing this was the most challenging endeavor I'd ever set out on academically, and I'm ready to take the skills I've been developing to a company which appreciates the value of data and the insights that are waiting to be found within. In my spare time I enjoy reading, coding, and getting cozy with my cats, and once it's safe I can't wait for the NYC Pinball league to start up again.
